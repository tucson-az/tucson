Arizona Sun Damage To Roofs

Insights from the Experts: Roofing Contractors Share Their Top Tips for Preventing Sun Damage in Arizona

You might not realize it, but the relentless Arizona sun can wreak havoc on your roof, regardless of its material. This unseen threat can damage different roofing materials in unique ways, from fading colors to weakening the overall structure.

The Arizona sun damage to roofs mainly occurs due to the state's high UV index and extreme temperatures, which can cause materials to expand and contract frequently, leading to cracks and fissures. For instance, asphalt shingles may become brittle and break, while wood shakes can warp or split. Even sturdy materials like tile and metal aren't immune to the sun's powerful rays.

Over time, these changes can compromise your home's integrity by allowing water to seep into the underlying structures, causing further damage. Understanding the impact on roofing materials is crucial for Arizona homeowners. Regular inspection and maintenance can help mitigate this damage, ensuring your roof can withstand the harsh sun and protect your home. So, don't underestimate the power of the Arizona sun; it's not just a beautiful sight but a potential threat to your roof's durability.

Under the Surface: How Underlayment and Roof Layers Suffer from Sun Damage. Interestingly, nearly 75% of roof repairs are due to underlayment failure, often exacerbated by the relentless heat exposure, silently compromising the layers of your roof. This under-the-surface damage is a stealthy assailant, slowly eroding your home's integrity.

The underlayment, a protective barrier installed between the roof's surface and its structural components, is typically hidden from sight. Nonetheless, it plays a critical role in maintaining the roof's structural integrity. Over time, the underlayment can suffer from sun damage, just like the visible parts of the roof. The Arizona sun's harsh rays can cause this crucial layer to dry out, become brittle, and eventually crack.

The resulting damage to the roof layers can be significant. Cracked underlayment allows water to seep into the structure, leading to further problems such as mold growth, wood rot, and even structural instability.

This is where the true impact of Arizona sun damage becomes evident. The brutal sun silently wreaks havoc on your roof, causing damage that's out of sight but should never be out of mind. It's imperative to regularly inspect and maintain your roof's underlayment to safeguard your home's integrity from this unseen threat.
